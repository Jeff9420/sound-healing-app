Privacy Policy

Last Updated: September 8, 2025

1. Introduction

Welcome to SoundFlow ("we," "our," or "us"). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website and use our sound healing application.

We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring you have a positive experience on our website and in using our services.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide Voluntarily

  • Contact Information: When you contact us, we may collect your name, email address, and message content.
  • Preferences: Language preferences and audio settings you choose are stored locally on your device.

2.2 Information Automatically Collected

  • Usage Data: We collect information about how you use our website, including pages visited, time spent, and features used.
  • Device Information: We may collect information about your device, including IP address, browser type, operating system, and screen resolution.
  • Cookies and Local Storage: We use cookies and local storage to remember your preferences and improve your experience.

2.3 Third-Party Analytics

  • Google Analytics: We use Google Analytics to understand how visitors interact with our website.
  • Google AdSense: We participate in Google AdSense to display relevant advertisements.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the collected information for the following purposes:

  • To provide, operate, and maintain our website and services
  • To improve, personalize, and expand our website and services
  • To understand and analyze how you use our website
  • To develop new products, services, features, and functionality
  • To communicate with you for customer service and support purposes
  • To display personalized advertisements through Google AdSense
  • To prevent fraud and enhance security

4. Information Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell, trade, or otherwise transfer your personal information to outside parties except as described below:

4.1 Service Providers

  • Google Analytics: For website analytics and performance monitoring
  • Google AdSense: For displaying advertisements
  • Vercel: For website hosting and performance

4.2 Legal Requirements

We may disclose your information if required to do so by law or in response to valid requests by public authorities.

5. Data Storage and Security

  • Local Storage: Your preferences are stored locally on your device and are not transmitted to our servers.
  • Data Security: We implement appropriate security measures to protect your information against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction.

6. Data Retention Policy

We are committed to retaining your personal information only as long as necessary for the purposes outlined in this policy.

6.1 Retention Periods by Data Type

  • User Preferences (Local Storage): Retained until you clear your browser data or uninstall the PWA
  • Analytics Data (Google Analytics): Retained for 14 months as per Google's default retention policy
  • IP Addresses: Anonymized after 2 weeks
  • Usage Data: Aggregated and anonymized after 30 days
  • Contact Information: Retained for 2 years from last contact, unless you request deletion
  • Cookie Data: Retained according to your browser settings and cookie preferences

6.2 Data Deletion Triggers

We will delete your personal information in the following circumstances:

  • When you exercise your right to deletion and request removal
  • When the data is no longer necessary for the purposes it was collected
  • When our retention period expires
  • When required by law or regulation
  • When the service is discontinued (with appropriate notice)

6.3 Automated Data Cleanup

Our system includes automated processes that:

  • Regularly review and delete outdated information
  • Anonymize or aggregate data when specific retention periods end
  • Implement secure deletion methods that prevent data recovery
  • Maintain logs of data deletion activities for audit purposes

6.4 Legal and Regulatory Compliance

We may retain certain data longer than standard retention periods when:

  • Required by law, regulation, or legal process
  • Necessary to establish, exercise, or defend legal claims
  • Required for compliance with financial or tax regulations
  • Essential for public health or safety reasons

6.5 Data Backups and Archiving

Maintained data backups follow these retention guidelines:

  • Active Backups: Retained for 30 days
  • Archive Backups: Retained for 1 year in encrypted, secure storage
  • Disaster Recovery: Critical data retained for business continuity only

7. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and similar tracking technologies to track activity on our website and store certain information.

Types of Cookies We Use:

  • Essential Cookies: Necessary for the website to function properly
  • Analytics Cookies: Help us understand how visitors interact with our website
  • Advertising Cookies: Used to display relevant advertisements
  • Preference Cookies: Remember your settings and preferences

You can choose to disable cookies through your browser settings, but this may affect the functionality of our website.
