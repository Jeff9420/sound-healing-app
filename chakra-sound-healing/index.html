Frequently Asked Questions

What are chakras and are they scientifically proven?

Chakras are energy centers described in ancient Vedic and yogic texts, corresponding to nerve plexuses and endocrine glands in the body. While Western medicine doesn't recognize "chakras" directly, research on biofield science, acupuncture meridians, and vagus nerve function provides parallel frameworks. Sound healing for chakras is best understood as a mind-body practice combining meditation, intention, and vibrational therapy ‚Äî all of which have documented physiological effects.

Do healing frequencies like 432Hz and 528Hz really work?

The scientific evidence is mixed. Some studies suggest 432Hz music reduces stress more than standard 440Hz tuning, while 528Hz has been studied for potential DNA repair (though findings are preliminary). What's clear: intention, relaxation, and focused listening create measurable benefits regardless of exact frequency. If a sound helps you meditate and focus healing intention, it's working.

How long does it take to balance chakras with sound?

Effects vary widely. Some people feel immediate shifts (relaxation, clarity, emotional release) within 10-20 minutes. For deep, lasting changes, consistent practice over 2-4 weeks is typical. Think of chakra work as ongoing maintenance, not a one-time fix. Daily 10-15 minute sessions for a specific chakra, then rotating through all 7, works well.

Should I work on one chakra or all 7?

Both approaches work. Targeted approach: If you have a specific issue (anxiety = root/solar plexus, relationship problems = heart), focus 10-20 minutes daily on that chakra until balanced. Sequential approach: Work through all 7 chakras in order (root to crown) for 5-10 minutes each for holistic alignment. Many practitioners do a weekly "full chakra cleanse" plus daily focus on problem areas.

Can I use singing bowls and solfeggio frequencies together?

Absolutely! Singing bowls and solfeggio tones complement each other. Bowls provide rich, multi-layered harmonics that engage the whole body, while pure solfeggio tones (396Hz, 528Hz, etc.) target specific chakras with precision. You can play them simultaneously or alternate ‚Äî use bowls to "open" a session, then solfeggio frequencies for focused healing, then bowls again to "seal" the work.
